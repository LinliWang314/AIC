Why it exists

AI can generate options. It still cannot decide what is worth keeping.

As AI becomes more capable of producing ideas, variations, and logical recommendations, a different human responsibility comes into focus: judgment. Not just what works, but what feels proportionate, thoughtful, and meaningful in context.

AIC explores that responsibility through art. It treats artistic practice not as decoration, but as one of the clearest places where taste, restraint, and interpretation still matter most.

What it is becoming

The format is intentionally unfinished.

Over three gatherings so far, AIC has moved from open-ended project discussions, to conversational explorations of AI-generated artworks, and then toward more structured creative sessions grounded in specific scenarios.

Iteration over scale

The form changes in response to feedback instead of being frozen into a repeatable event template.

Thoughtfulness over throughput

AIC optimizes for creativity, reflection, and genuine enjoyment, not maximum attendance or output.

Get Involved

AIC is not optimized for scale. It is open to thoughtful people.

Future gatherings at GH will remain small and intentional. If the premise resonates, the best next step is simply to start a conversation.

This can become a place for collaborators, artists, engineers, researchers, and designers who want a slower way to explore AI and creation together.

What to expect
